ARVR

Virtual reality (VR) and its increasingly popular cousin, augmented reality (AR), are two of the most popular tech trends today.

Virtual Reality is defined as “the use of computer technology to create a simulated environment.” With a VR headset, your entire environment is replaced by whatever the headset is projecting, allowing you to view a completely different reality than the one in front of you. You can move around and look in every direction, as if you were physically there.

ARVR will radically change how we work, learn, exercise, communicate and consume content.

5 things to know about AR/VR

Here are five things about these two technologies to keep in mind.

  • AR should not be confused with VR
  • At its core, VR is not as high-tech as we think
  • You have to see before you believe
  • VR goes beyond games
  • AR also for assistive or rehabilitative purposes
